Math Fallacy Solution

In general, division of f(x) on both sides where by there exists at least one solution such that f(x) = 0 omits existing solutions in the new equation while for multiplication, it introduces new solutions in the new equation.

In substitution steps, it is more difficult to see such errors and the only way to determine if such an error is made is if it is converted into the context of an elimination step like what was done above.
